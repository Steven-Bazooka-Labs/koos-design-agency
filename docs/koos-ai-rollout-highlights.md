# koos-ai — Rollout at a Glance

*Bazooka Labs · July 2026 · The one-page version — the full plan (phases, costs, data handling, risks, criteria) is in the accompanying rollout plan.*

## Ready today

- **The shared toolkit is built and pilot-ready (v0.3).** The three tools we agreed on — **Contract Checker**, **Survey Builder**, **Design Token Generator** — are complete, original rule files included, and grounded in real Koos content (brand voice, company profile, your contract baseline).
- **The operating model works:** designers install once with two commands; improvements ship as versioned releases; the release→update loop is **verified end-to-end, including in the desktop app**. Automatic pickup on your designers' machines is documented behaviour that we confirm in Week 1 — with a verified 30-second manual fallback.
- **The improvement loop is live** — the repo ships with a feedback log seeded with real Build Week feedback, including two small T&C updates for your legal team that the Contract Checker surfaced.
- **Four guides ship with the repo:** setup, designer onboarding, the steward handbook, and a one-pager on how updates reach everyone.

## The proposal: one pilot, two tracks, 3 weeks, on live work

- **Track A — contracts:** every incoming contract goes through the Contract Checker, run by the people who actually handle contracts; anything tool-drafted goes out **only after your commercial owner's review**.
- **Track B — design & research:** 2–3 designers use the Survey Builder / Token Generator as their live projects need them.

Week 1 onboard (and confirm auto-update on your machines) · Week 2 use, with at least one improvement shipped mid-pilot · Week 3 measure against the criteria **you judge** — then expand, adjust, or stop.

The decision that matters most: **the steward(s)** — the git-comfortable people at Koos who own the **canon** (the shared, versioned home of the tools and Koos knowledge), triage feedback, and ship releases. We recommend naming **two**, at 2–4 h/week each during the pilot, with an internal booking code so the time is real. Bazooka sets them up and coaches their first releases.

## The two things we put on the table before anything runs live

- **Money:** Bazooka effort is an estimated **[10–14 h] for Phase 0** and **[16–22 h] for the pilot** [rates and totals in the accompanying engagement note]; your internal cost is **~25–40 hours**, mostly steward time, plus pilot Claude seats. **If Week 3 says stop, that's the whole exposure — and the working toolkit, repo and guides are yours regardless.**
- **Client data:** a **Phase-0 data gate** before any live document goes through a tool — Koos-owned Claude workspace on commercial terms, the data-processing agreement, a sweep of pilot clients' contracts for AI clauses, and one legal-approved sentence everyone can say when a client asks. Pilot metrics are aggregated and self-reported only — these are assistive tools, not monitoring — and the wider team is briefed before any agency-wide rollout.

## What we need from Koos to start (Phase 0 — the week after you confirm)

1. A Koos **GitHub organisation** to own the repo (we hand everything over — it's yours, in writing).
2. **Steward name(s)** + a 2-hour setup session.
3. **Track A owner + 2–3 designers** and the pilot project(s).
4. A **60–90 min guardrails working session** with your research leads — your research-ethics practice goes in, your edits become v1.
5. **Legal capacity** for the data gate, and a **#koos-ai** Slack channel.

## How we'll know it worked (judged by you, not by us)

≥3 live contracts through the tool, with one reviewed amendment actually sent · time-per-review vs. a pre-pilot baseline, target set by you at kickoff · ≥2 real design/research-tool runs whose outputs were used in live project work · ≥2 improvements shipped from your team's feedback, at least one picked up on every pilot machine mid-pilot · clear operational verdicts (Google Drive workflow, update reliability) · an evidence-based expand/adjust/stop decision.

## After the pilot

Onboard the next wave with the same guides, and promote the remaining Build Week tools one release at a time — transcript analysis, token checking, proposals, case studies — plus sector guardrails, starting with healthcare. The lead-gen and AI-search-visibility ideas stay parked until their open questions (deliverability, GDPR, B-Corp fit) are answered — flagged, not forgotten.
