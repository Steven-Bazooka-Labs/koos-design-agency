# Stakeholder Meeting Notes

## Meeting: Kick-off Alignment -- Multimodal Journey Planner Evaluation
## Date: 2026-03-18
## Attendees: Product Owner (NS App), Head of Customer Experience, Journey Management Lead, Engineering Lead, Koos Research Lead, Koos Project Lead

---

## Key Positions

**Product Team** views the multimodal planner as the key differentiator versus Google Maps. Their argument: Google Maps gives you directions, but the NS app gives you real-time OV-chipkaart integration, platform-specific departure information, and disruption handling that Google cannot match. The Product Owner stated plainly: "If we can't prove this is better than Google Maps for Dutch public transport, we have a problem." The team wants research to validate the product's value proposition and identify specific UX improvements for the next sprint cycle.

**Marketing** needs success stories for the NS annual report (due Q3 2026). They want quotable passenger feedback and usage metrics that demonstrate the planner's impact. There is pressure to show that NS is innovating, particularly given public criticism of train punctuality in recent months. Marketing's ideal outcome is a narrative: "passengers love it, here's how we're making travel easier."

**Customer Experience** is more cautious. The CX team suspects a discoverability problem. Their data shows that passengers who use the planner rate the app significantly higher, but most passengers do not know the feature exists. The Head of CX raised the question: "Are we solving a problem passengers didn't know they had, or are we just not telling them about it well enough?" She wants the research to separate product quality from product awareness.

**Engineering** is proud of the real-time data integration. The Engineering Lead walked through the technical achievement: pulling live departure data from nine different transit operators, normalizing it, and presenting it in a unified interface with sub-minute accuracy. The team wants feedback on whether the real-time information is perceived as reliable and whether transfer times feel accurate in practice.

## Agreed Research Scope

The group agreed to research a mix of passenger types: daily commuters, occasional travelers, and tourists. There was healthy debate about whether to include passengers who have never used the planner. Decision: include both users and non-users, with an emphasis on first-encounter reactions from those who have not tried it yet.

## Underlying Tension

There is a visible tension between "celebrate the launch" (Marketing's priority) and "find out what's wrong" (Product and CX's priority). The Koos team clarified its role: the research will be honest and evidence-based. If the planner works well, the evidence will show that. If there are problems, those need to surface now before a wider rollout. Both the Product Owner and Head of CX endorsed this framing. Marketing accepted it, but noted they still need usable material for the annual report.
