# Desk Research -- Multimodal Journey Planner Context

## Compiled: 2026-03-20
## Sources: NS internal data (shared under NDA), App Store / Google Play reviews, industry reports, competitor analysis

---

## NS App Usage Statistics

The NS app is one of the most widely installed apps in the Netherlands. As of early 2026, the app has been downloaded approximately 8.2 million times across iOS and Android. Monthly active users hover around 3.4 million. The app's primary use cases remain checking train times (78% of sessions), purchasing tickets (41%), and viewing disruption information (33%). These figures reflect a user base that treats the NS app as a train-specific tool.

The multimodal journey planner, launched in November 2025, has been accessed by approximately 510,000 unique users -- roughly 15% of the monthly active user base. Of those who tried it, approximately 38% returned for a second use. Retention drops significantly after the third use among commuters but remains relatively stable among occasional travelers. The planner is accessed most frequently on weekends and during holiday periods, suggesting it serves non-routine travel rather than daily commute patterns.

The data suggests an inverse relationship between travel frequency and planner adoption. Commuters who know their route do not feel a need for multimodal planning. Tourists and occasional travelers, who face more uncertainty, show the highest engagement.

## Competitor Landscape

**Google Maps** remains the dominant journey planning tool in the Netherlands for multimodal trips. Google Maps covers all Dutch public transport operators and includes cycling, walking, and driving. Its advantage: ubiquity. Almost every smartphone user already has it. Its weakness: it does not integrate with OV-chipkaart, does not show real-time NS platform assignments, and provides generic disruption information rather than operator-specific alternatives.

**9292** is the official Dutch public transport journey planner, operated by NDOV (Nationale Data Openbaar Vervoer). It covers all operators and is widely trusted for bus, tram, and metro information. However, 9292's app has lower ratings (3.2 stars on Google Play vs. NS's 4.4) and a dated interface. Many users describe it as functional but not pleasant to use. The NS planner's main opportunity is to absorb 9292's multimodal utility into a better-designed, more trusted app.

## NS Strategic Vision

NS's 2025-2030 strategy document positions the company as a "mobility partner" rather than a railway operator. The multimodal journey planner is listed as a strategic priority alongside NS Flex, international ticketing, and shared mobility partnerships (OV-fiets, car sharing). The vision: a single NS app that covers every trip a passenger takes, regardless of which operator runs each leg. The journey planner is the first visible expression of this vision in the consumer product.

The review pattern reinforces the internal data: passengers who discover and use the planner tend to value it, but discoverability and data accuracy for non-train modes are recurring pain points.
