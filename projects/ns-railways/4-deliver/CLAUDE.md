# Phase Context -- Deliver

## Phase Purpose

Evaluative research on the recently launched multimodal journey planner. This phase assesses whether the live product meets passenger needs and identifies specific improvements before wider rollout. The planner is in the market -- this is not concept testing but real-product evaluation.

## Research Approach

Evaluative research. Structured interviews with passengers who have and have not used the multimodal journey planner. Combines retrospective experience assessment (for existing users) with first-encounter observation (for non-users). Validation of whether the product delivers on its intended value proposition.

## Key Questions

- Are passengers aware that the multimodal journey planner exists in the NS app?
- Does the planner improve the journey planning experience compared to alternatives?
- Where does the planner fall short of passenger expectations?
- Would passengers recommend the planner to others?
- How does the planner compare to Google Maps and 9292 for different journey types?

## Analysis Focus

This is evaluative, not exploratory. The product exists and has been launched. Focus on what works, what does not work, and what needs iteration. Assess the product against the original design intent from earlier phases. Surface concrete, actionable improvements rather than abstract opportunity areas.

Specific areas to evaluate:

- **Discoverability** -- Can passengers find the multimodal planner within the NS app without guidance?
- **Comprehension** -- Do passengers understand the multimodal view and how to read combined itineraries?
- **Trust** -- Do passengers trust the real-time data for non-train modes (bus, tram, metro)?
- **Comparison** -- How does the experience compare to Google Maps and 9292 in the passenger's own assessment?
- **Disruption handling** -- Does the planner provide useful guidance when part of a multimodal trip is disrupted?
- **Recommendation intent** -- Would passengers actively tell others about this feature?

## Participant Mix

10 train passengers: 5 daily commuters, 3 occasional travelers, 2 tourists (interviewed in English). Recruited via NS customer panel and station intercepts. See `scoping.md` for full details.

## Constraints

- Tourist interviews conducted in English; all others in Dutch
- Commuters recruited via NS customer panel -- these are heavier NS users than average, which may skew positively
- Station intercepts for occasional travelers and tourists introduce self-selection bias toward passengers already at NS stations
