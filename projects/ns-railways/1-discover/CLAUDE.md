# Phase Context -- Discover

## Phase Purpose

Exploratory research to understand how passengers currently plan and experience multimodal journeys in the Netherlands. This phase casts a wide net: how do different passenger types navigate trips that involve multiple transport modes? What tools do they use? Where do they struggle? What workarounds have they developed?

## Research Approach

Generative research. Open-ended interviews with passengers across a range of travel patterns, exploring their current journey planning behaviors, pain points, and unmet needs. No product-specific evaluation at this stage -- the focus is on understanding the problem space before examining NS's solution.

## Key Questions

- How do passengers currently plan trips that involve more than one transport mode?
- What tools, apps, and information sources do they rely on?
- Where do passengers experience friction, confusion, or stress in multimodal journeys?
- How do passengers handle disruptions when their trip spans multiple operators?
- What would an ideal journey planning experience look like from the passenger's perspective?

## Analysis Focus

Look for patterns in journey planning behavior, unmet needs, and emotional pain points. Pay attention to differences between passenger segments (commuters vs. occasional travelers vs. tourists). Surface workarounds -- these often reveal the strongest unmet needs.
