# Client Brief -- Municipality of Amsterdam

## From: Dienst Publiekszaken (Citizen Services Department), Gemeente Amsterdam
## Date: 2026-03-09

---

## Background

The Municipality of Amsterdam serves approximately 850,000 residents across eight stadsdelen (boroughs). Our citizens interact with the municipality for permits, civil affairs, social services, waste management, public space maintenance, and dozens of other services. In recent years, we have invested significantly in digital channels -- our website, the MijnAmsterdam portal, and various service-specific applications.

However, digital adoption remains uneven. While some citizens manage all their municipal interactions online, others rely entirely on our service desks, telephone lines, or community-based support. Our service desks are overwhelmed. Wait times have increased. Staff report that many in-person visits are from citizens who started a process online but got stuck or confused. At the same time, citizens who prefer or require in-person service find it harder to get timely help because of the volume.

## Problem Statement

We want to improve citizen satisfaction with municipal services, but we do not want to assume we know where to start. Our digital channels exist, but adoption is uneven. Our service desks are under pressure. Citizens tell us that processes are too complex, that they do not understand what is expected of them, and that they rarely receive feedback on the status of their requests. We need to hear directly from citizens before deciding where to invest.

## Amsterdam's Context

Amsterdam is one of Europe's most linguistically and culturally diverse cities. Over 180 nationalities are represented. A significant portion of our residents are first- or second-generation immigrants, and many interact with the municipality in their most vulnerable moments -- applying for social assistance, registering a birth, navigating permits for housing modifications. We have a political commitment to digital inclusion: "no one left behind" in the digital transition. This is not a slogan. It is a policy directive from the College van B&W (Board of Mayor and Aldermen).

## What We Are Asking KOOS To Do

We want KOOS to conduct exploratory research with citizens to understand their lived experience of municipal services. We are specifically interested in three service domains: permits (vergunningen), reporting public space issues (meldingen), and social services (sociale dienstverlening). We want to understand friction points, workarounds, emotional experiences, and unmet needs. We want to hear from citizens of diverse backgrounds -- age, digital literacy, language, neighborhood, and immigration background.

We are open to where the findings lead. We are not looking for validation of a predetermined solution. We want the evidence to guide our next steps.
