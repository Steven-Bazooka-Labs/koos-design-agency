## Key Decisions

**Service domain focus:** The group agreed on three domains for the research: permits (vergunningen), reporting issues in public space (meldingen openbare ruimte), and social services (sociale dienstverlening). These were selected because they represent high volume, high complexity, and high emotional stakes respectively.

**Research participants:** The municipality wants research to include citizens of diverse backgrounds. Specifically: a range of ages, digital literacy levels, neighborhoods, and immigration backgrounds. This is non-negotiable -- findings that only represent digitally fluent, native Dutch speakers are not useful for policy.

**Openness to change:** Marieke confirmed the municipality is open to radical change if the evidence supports it. "We are not here to defend our current systems. If the research says we need to start over, we are willing to hear that."

## What People Said

**Wethouder El Idrissi:** "We have elections in March 2027. I need to show citizens that we are listening and making progress. I do not need a full transformation by then, but I need credible quick wins alongside a longer-term roadmap. The research needs to give us both -- things we can act on immediately and a strategic direction."

**Tom Bakker (Digital Team):** "Our biggest challenge is not technology. It is that we have 40 different systems that do not talk to each other. Citizens experience this as chaos. I want this research to build the case for systemic integration, not just another front-end redesign."

**Sandra Okonkwo (Service Desk):** "My team handles 800 to 1,000 contacts per day. At least a third are people who tried online and got stuck. Another third are people who do not trust the online system and want a human to confirm things are being handled. We are exhausted. Whatever comes out of this research -- please make sure it actually reduces pressure on the front line."

**Marieke de Vries:** "We have done surveys before. Citizens say they are 'moderately satisfied.' But that number hides enormous variation. Some people have no issues at all. Others are completely lost. I want this research to surface the stories behind the numbers."
