## Research Questions

1. What are the main friction points citizens experience when accessing municipal services (permits, reporting, social services)?
2. What workarounds have citizens developed to navigate municipal processes that do not work well for them?
3. Where are the biggest gaps between what citizens need and what the municipality currently offers?
4. How does the experience differ for citizens with different levels of digital literacy?
5. What moments in the service journey have the most emotional impact -- positive or negative?

## Interview Guide Summary

- General municipal service usage: which services, how often, through which channels
- Specific recent experiences: walk through a recent interaction with the municipality step by step
- Digital vs. physical channel preferences: what drives the choice, what works, what does not
- Frustrations and pain points: what goes wrong, what causes stress or confusion
- Satisfactions: what works well, what feels easy or positive
- Workarounds: unofficial strategies citizens use to get things done when the official process fails them
- Ideal experience: what would good municipal service look like from the citizen's perspective

---

## Known Constraints or Biases

- Recruitment bias: Participants recruited through community centers and the municipality's citizen panel. Citizens who are fully disengaged from community life and municipal processes are likely underrepresented. This is the hardest-to-reach group and potentially the most underserved.
- Language: All interviews conducted in Dutch. Citizens who do not speak Dutch are excluded from this round. This is a significant limitation given Amsterdam's linguistic diversity.
- Sample size: 10 participants -- sufficient for qualitative pattern identification, not for quantification or statistical generalization.
- Service domain focus: Research focuses on permits, reporting, and social services. Other municipal services (waste, civil affairs, parking) are out of scope but may come up organically.
- Moderator consistency: Lina Zhao conducting all interviews to ensure consistency, but this means a single moderator's style influences all sessions.

## Additional Context

This project builds on KOOS's prior relationship with the Municipality of Amsterdam. Previous work included inclusive digital services (Service Design Award 2020 Finalist) and teacher shortage reduction (18.7% to 12.2%). The municipality trusts KOOS and is genuinely open to wherever the evidence leads.

Political context matters: municipal elections are in March 2027. The Wethouder (Alderman) for Digitalization needs both quick wins and a strategic roadmap. The research should surface opportunities at both levels -- immediate improvements and systemic changes.

Key desk research findings to keep in mind during analysis: permit satisfaction is 5.8/10 and declining, 42% of online permit applications are abandoned, social services has a 61% digital drop-off rate, and the gap between MijnAmsterdam registrations (412,000) and active users (138,000) suggests usability and trust issues rather than awareness problems.
