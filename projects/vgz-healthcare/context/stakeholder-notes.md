# Stakeholder Notes -- Concept Development Workshop

**Date:** 2026-02-12
**Attendees:** Director of Digital Innovation, Product Owner (Patient Portal), Head of Customer Experience, two Medical Advisors, KOOS project team (3 people)
**Location:** VGZ headquarters, Arnhem

---

## Workshop Purpose

Align on the portal concept scope and discuss the clickable prototype before concept testing sessions with patients. Four features have been developed into a clickable prototype (Figma) for testing.

## Features in Prototype

1. **Claims tracker** -- Dashboard showing claim status with timeline visualization. Filter by date, type, and status. Push notifications for status changes.
2. **Medication overview** -- Current prescriptions, pharmacy details, renewal reminders. Option to request repeat prescriptions directly.
3. **Care provider finder** -- Map-based search with filters for specialty, distance, waiting time, and contract status. Shows estimated out-of-pocket costs per provider.
4. **Referral letter upload** -- Photo capture or PDF upload of referral letters. Status tracking from submission through to specialist appointment confirmation.

## Key Discussion Points

### Digital Literacy Concerns

The Head of Customer Experience raised concerns about digital literacy among elderly policyholders. VGZ's policyholder base skews older than competitors like CZ -- roughly 28% of VGZ policyholders are over 65. The prototype assumes smartphone or desktop competence. A significant portion of this demographic currently relies on phone support. If the portal replaces phone channels without adequate alternatives, VGZ risks alienating its most vulnerable customers.

The Product Owner acknowledged this but argued that the portal should be designed for the majority and that phone support would remain available alongside digital options.

### Trust and Medical Data

Both medical advisors expressed caution about patient self-management features, particularly medication management. Displaying prescription data and enabling repeat medication requests raises questions about data accuracy, liability if a patient acts on outdated information, and the risk of patients bypassing GP oversight. They recommended clear disclaimers and integration with the GP information system (HIS) to ensure data freshness.

The Director of Digital Innovation noted that trust is the foundational requirement. If patients do not trust the portal with their health data, adoption will stall regardless of feature quality. VGZ's recent privacy incidents (a data breach affecting 2,300 policyholders in 2024) may still affect perceptions.

### Rollout Strategy

The team debated phased rollout versus big bang launch. The Product Owner favoured launching all four features simultaneously to create a "complete" experience. The medical advisors and Head of CX advocated phased rollout -- starting with claims tracking (lowest risk, highest volume) and adding features based on adoption data. KOOS recommended phased rollout to allow iterative learning, which aligns with the concept testing approach: if testing reveals that certain features do not resonate, they can be deprioritized before launch.

### Success Metrics

The group agreed on preliminary success metrics: reduction in call volume for claims-related queries (target: 25% reduction within 12 months), portal adoption rate (target: 40% of active policyholders within 6 months), and NPS improvement (target: +5 points within 12 months).

## Open Questions for Concept Testing

- Do patients actually want medication management from their insurer, or do they see this as the pharmacy's or GP's domain?
- How important is the care provider finder compared to simply asking their GP?
- Will patients trust digital referral letter submission, or will they worry about letters getting lost?
- What would make elderly policyholders comfortable using the portal?
