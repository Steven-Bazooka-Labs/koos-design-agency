# Desk Research -- VGZ Digital Self-Service Portal

## Dutch Health Insurance Market Overview

The Netherlands operates a mandatory health insurance system introduced by the Zorgverzekeringswet (Health Insurance Act) of 2006. Every resident must purchase basic health insurance (basisverzekering) from a private insurer. The government defines the basic package, which covers GP visits, hospital care, mental health treatment, prescription medication, and maternity care. Insurers compete on price, service, and supplementary insurance packages (aanvullende verzekering) that cover dental, physiotherapy, and alternative medicine.

There are approximately 10 active health insurers in the Netherlands, consolidated into four major groups: Zilveren Kruis (Achmea), VGZ, CZ, and Menzis. Together they cover roughly 90% of the Dutch population. Switching between insurers is allowed annually during the open enrolment period (November--December), with approximately 6-7% of the population switching each year.

## VGZ's Market Position

Cooperatie VGZ is the second-largest insurer group, covering approximately 4.2 million lives through its brands (VGZ, Univé, UMC Zorgverzekering, IZZ, and others). As a cooperative, VGZ returns surplus to members rather than shareholders. VGZ has historically positioned itself as a care-oriented insurer with strong regional roots, particularly in the south and east of the Netherlands.

### Customer Satisfaction (Fictional but Realistic)

- **NPS:** +14 (industry average: +8). Improved from +1 following KOOS's previous service redesign engagement.
- **Overall satisfaction:** 7.4 out of 10 (industry average: 7.1).
- **Digital channel satisfaction:** 6.8 out of 10 -- notably lower than overall satisfaction, indicating a gap between digital and non-digital experiences.
- **Phone service satisfaction:** 8.1 out of 10 -- VGZ's strongest channel, which creates a paradox: the channel VGZ wants to reduce is the one patients rate highest.

## Digital Adoption Among Health Insurance Customers

According to industry benchmarks and VGZ's internal data:

- 72% of policyholders under 50 prefer digital channels for simple transactions (claims submission, policy changes).
- 38% of policyholders over 65 have used the VGZ app at least once; only 14% use it regularly.
- Mobile app usage has grown 23% year-over-year, but active usage plateaus after initial download -- most users open the app only when submitting a claim.
- The most common digital action is claims submission (photo of receipt). Beyond this, digital engagement drops sharply.

## Competitor Digital Offerings

### Zilveren Kruis (Achmea)

Market leader. Launched a redesigned app in 2024 with claims tracking, digital health coach, and medication reminders. Early adoption data suggests strong uptake among younger policyholders but limited engagement from over-55s. No care provider finder yet. Received mixed reviews for overly complex navigation.

### CZ

Third-largest insurer. Offers a streamlined app focused on claims and policy management. Recently introduced a care provider search feature with waiting time estimates. CZ's strategy prioritizes simplicity over feature breadth. Customer satisfaction with CZ's app is high (7.6 out of 10), partly because it attempts less and therefore confuses less.

### Menzis

Smallest of the big four. Offers basic digital self-service. Notable for a pilot programme allowing digital GP consultations through the insurer's platform, blurring the line between insurer and care provider.

## Known Pain Points from Existing Feedback Channels

VGZ's customer insights team compiled the following recurring pain points from call centre data, complaint logs, and the annual customer survey:

1. **Medication switching frustration (preferentiebeleid)** -- VGZ's preferred medication policy means patients are periodically switched to a different brand of the same generic medication. Patients experience this as disruptive and confusing, especially elderly patients managing multiple prescriptions. This is the single largest source of complaints.

2. **Claims process opacity** -- Patients submit claims but receive no confirmation or status update until reimbursement appears (or does not appear) in their bank account. The gap between submission and resolution creates anxiety, particularly for larger claims like dental work or physiotherapy.

3. **Referral letter hassle** -- The process of getting a referral letter from GP to specialist to insurer involves multiple handoffs, often mixing digital and paper channels. Letters get lost, arrive late, or are sent to the wrong department. Patients frequently call to check whether their referral has been received.

4. **Care provider coverage confusion** -- Patients struggle to determine whether a specific provider is contracted by VGZ under their policy. Choosing a non-contracted provider can result in significantly higher out-of-pocket costs. The current coverage checker on VGZ's website is difficult to use and not integrated into the care search journey.

5. **Lack of proactive communication** -- Patients feel VGZ only communicates when it needs something (premium payments, policy renewals) rather than when the patient needs something (claim updates, coverage clarifications, health tips).
