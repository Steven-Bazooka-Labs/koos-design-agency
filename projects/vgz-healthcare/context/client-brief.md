# Client Brief -- VGZ Digital Self-Service Portal

## Background

Cooperatie VGZ insures approximately 4.2 million people in the Netherlands. Within the Dutch healthcare system, every resident is legally required to hold basic health insurance (basisverzekering), and insurers like VGZ act as intermediaries between patients and care providers. Insurers negotiate contracts with hospitals, GPs, pharmacies, and specialists, and reimburse claims on behalf of policyholders. Patients interact with their insurer primarily when submitting declarations, managing their policy, or navigating the care system -- moments that are often stressful, confusing, or time-sensitive.

Dutch patients increasingly expect the same level of digital convenience from their health insurer that they experience in banking, travel, and retail. Yet health insurance remains a sector where most interactions still require phone calls, postal mail, or navigating disconnected digital channels.

## The Opportunity

VGZ wants to build a unified digital self-service portal that gives policyholders meaningful control over their health administration. The portal concept includes four core features:

1. **Claims status tracking** -- Real-time visibility into submitted claims: what has been received, what is being processed, what has been approved or rejected, and expected reimbursement timelines. Currently, policyholders call VGZ's service centre for status updates -- this accounts for a significant share of inbound call volume.

2. **Medication management** -- An overview of current prescriptions, repeat medication requests, and notifications when medication is due for renewal. This is especially relevant for the chronically ill population managing multiple prescriptions.

3. **Care provider finder** -- A searchable directory of contracted care providers, filtered by specialty, location, waiting times, and whether the provider is covered under the policyholder's specific policy. Choosing the wrong provider can result in unexpected out-of-pocket costs, which is a major source of patient frustration.

4. **Referral letter digital submission** -- The ability to upload and track referral letters (verwijsbrieven) digitally rather than relying on fax or postal delivery between GP, specialist, and insurer. The current referral process is opaque and slow.

## VGZ's Hypothesis

VGZ believes patients want more control and transparency. Internal data shows that over 60% of inbound phone calls concern questions that could be answered through self-service: claim status, provider coverage, and referral tracking. Reducing these calls through a well-designed portal would simultaneously improve patient experience and reduce operational cost.

## Cost Pressure

Like all Dutch health insurers, VGZ faces rising healthcare costs and thinning margins on basic insurance. Digital transformation is partly a strategic imperative (meeting patient expectations) and partly an economic necessity (reducing service cost per policyholder). The portal must deliver on both fronts to justify the investment.

## What VGZ Is Asking Koos

Before committing to full development, VGZ wants to validate the portal concept with real patients. The central question: does this concept genuinely address patient needs, or is VGZ solving for its own operational efficiency and projecting that onto patients?
