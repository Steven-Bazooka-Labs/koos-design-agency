# Phase 3: Develop -- VGZ Healthcare

## Double Diamond Context

The Develop phase is the second divergent stage of the Double Diamond. With the problem clearly defined, the goal is now to develop and test potential solutions. This phase involves prototyping, co-creation, and evaluative research -- putting concepts in front of real users to learn what works, what fails, and what is missing.

## Phase Status

**Active.**

## What Is Happening

Concept testing of a digital self-service portal with VGZ policyholders. A clickable Figma prototype covering four features (claims tracking, medication management, care provider finder, referral letter upload) is being shown to 10 participants in semi-structured interviews. The research is evaluative: the aim is to test specific hypotheses about the portal concept against identified patient needs.

## Research Focus

1. **Concept-need alignment** -- Does the portal concept address the needs surfaced in Discover and prioritized in Define?
2. **Feature evaluation** -- Which features generate genuine enthusiasm and which fall flat? What is the perceived value hierarchy?
3. **Inclusivity and accessibility** -- Does the concept work across the full range of VGZ's policyholder base, including elderly, chronically ill, and digitally less confident users?
4. **Trust and data sensitivity** -- Are patients comfortable managing health data through a portal operated by their insurer? What are the trust barriers?
5. **Gap identification** -- What needs are not addressed by the current concept? What would patients add or change?

## Key Documents

- `scoping.md` -- Research scope, questions, hypotheses, and participant composition
- `transcripts/` -- Individual interview transcripts (to be added as interviews are conducted)
- `analysis/` -- Analysis outputs produced using Koos protocols

## Analysis Guidance

When running analyses on Develop phase transcripts:

- Evaluate findings against the portal concept specifically. This is not open-ended exploration -- it is evaluative research.
- Cross-reference findings with the scoping document's hypotheses. Explicitly confirm, nuance, or reject each hypothesis.
- Pay particular attention to differences across participant segments (chronic condition patients, elderly, young families, etc.).
- Flag any findings that suggest the portal concept may cause harm -- for example, if self-management features could lead patients to bypass necessary clinical oversight.
- Note where participant reactions diverge from VGZ stakeholder assumptions documented in `context/stakeholder-notes.md`.
