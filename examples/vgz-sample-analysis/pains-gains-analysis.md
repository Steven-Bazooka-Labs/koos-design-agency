# Pains & Gains -- Analysis Output

---

## Executive Summary

> This analysis identifies 11 pains and 7 gains across three confidence tiers, drawn from 10 concept testing interviews with VGZ policyholders. The strongest pain is medication switching without adequate communication (19/20 runs), experienced by all 10 participants either personally or through a family member. This pain is both functional (confusing process) and deeply emotional (fear, anxiety about safety). The strongest gain is real-time claim status visibility (18/20), which participants compared to package tracking. A critical pain-gain connection runs through the entire dataset: the portal successfully addresses the information deficit pain but fails to address the action deficit pain -- participants can see problems but cannot resolve them. The prioritization matrix highlights three critical items: medication switching communication, claims opacity, and the absence of action capabilities. Two gains emerge as design principles rather than features: the feeling of independence/competence, and the reassurance that comes from proactive communication. The analysis surfaced 134 supporting quotes, of which 77% were verified as exact matches to transcript text.

## Methodology Note

This analysis was produced using Koos's multi-agent analysis protocol. Each transcript was analyzed independently by 20 agents across 4 interpretive lenses:

- **Obvious lens (5 agents):** Surface-level, explicitly stated pains and gains
- **Implicit lens (5 agents):** Pains and gains inferred from behavior, workarounds, and context
- **Emotional lens (5 agents):** Pains rooted in frustration, anxiety, or identity; gains rooted in delight, confidence, or belonging
- **Adversarial lens (5 agents):** Challenges, stress-tests, and alternative interpretations of proposed findings

Findings were then consolidated through convergence analysis. Confidence tiers reflect how many of the 20 independent runs surfaced each finding. All quotes were verified against source transcripts and all inferences were audited for evidentiary support.

Total agent runs for this analysis: 43 (20 lens runs + convergence + disagreement deep-dive + 15 verification runs).

## Recommended Next Steps

1. **Address PAIN-001 (medication switching) with redesigned communication** -- replace jargon with plain language, add advance notification (two weeks before change), and include a link to trusted medical information. This is the most severe, most universal, and most emotionally charged pain in the dataset.
2. **Launch GAIN-001 (claims tracking) as the MVP's lead feature** -- it directly resolves PAIN-002, is universally desired, and is the most technically straightforward to implement.
3. **Plan action capabilities (resolving PAIN-003) for version 2** -- the portal's credibility depends on moving from "view-only" to "interactive." Without this, participants rate the concept as a 6/10 improvement at best.
4. **Commission a plain-language review of all portal copy** -- PAIN-004 is not a minor usability issue. "Preferentiebeleid" actively erodes trust and triggers anxiety. Every piece of text visible to patients should be reviewed by a plain-language specialist.
5. **Design GAIN-005 (co-browsing support) as a bridging service** -- this addresses the digital divide without requiring the portal itself to be simpler. A phone agent who can see the patient's screen provides human reassurance alongside digital efficiency.
