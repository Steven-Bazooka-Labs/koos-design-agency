# Verification Report -- VGZ Healthcare Concept Testing Analysis

---

## Overview

This report documents the verification process applied to all analysis outputs for the VGZ digital self-service portal concept testing study. Verification was conducted by 5 independent verification agents, each checking all cited quotes against source transcripts and all inferences against available evidence.

### Methodology

Each of the 5 verification agents independently compared every cited quote against the source transcript. A quote was classified as:

- **Exact Match:** The cited text matches the transcript verbatim, allowing for minor punctuation differences.
- **Paraphrase (accurate):** The cited text captures the participant's meaning accurately but uses slightly different words, condensed phrasing, or a combined statement from two nearby utterances.
- **Out of Context:** The quote is real but the analysis uses it to support a finding that extends beyond what the participant intended in context.
- **Not Found:** The quoted text does not appear in any source transcript, even as a paraphrase.

### Paraphrased Quote Corrections

The following quotes were flagged as paraphrases during verification. The original transcript text is provided for accuracy.

**Example 1 -- JTBD Analysis, JOB-004**

- **Cited as:** "Als ik hier moet uitloggen en opnieuw inloggen om Fenna's spullen te zien, dan is het al niet meer handig."
- **Actual transcript (P03):** "Als ik hier moet uitloggen en opnieuw inloggen om Fenna's spullen te zien, dan is het al niet meer handig. Dan is het twee keer de helft, in plaats van een keer het geheel."
- **Assessment:** Accurate paraphrase -- the cited portion is a verbatim extract from a longer statement. The second sentence adds color but does not change the meaning. Classified as exact in most agent runs; two agents classified as paraphrase due to the truncation.

**Example 2 -- Pains & Gains Analysis, PAIN-001**

- **Cited as:** "Drie maanden lang pingpongde ik tussen huisarts, apotheek en verzekeraar, en ondertussen had ik maagklachten."
- **Actual transcript (P04):** "Drie maanden, drie maanden lang pingpongde ik tussen huisarts, apotheek en verzekeraar, en ondertussen had ik maagklachten."
- **Assessment:** Minor truncation of the repeated "drie maanden." Meaning preserved. Classified as paraphrase by 3 agents, exact by 2.

**Example 3 -- Journey Map, Stage 5**

- **Cited as:** "Waarom staat deze bovenaan? Is dat de beste? De dichtstbijzijnde? De populairste?"
- **Actual transcript (P03):** "Maar ik snap de volgorde niet. Waarom staat deze bovenaan? Is dat de beste? De dichtstbijzijnde? De populairste?"
- **Assessment:** Opening context sentence omitted. The cited portion is verbatim. Classified as paraphrase because the omitted sentence provides important framing (participant explicitly says they do not understand the ordering, which is stronger than the cited series of questions alone).

### Out-of-Context Expansions

The following quotes were flagged as used out of context. The original context is provided, along with the corrected interpretation.

**Example 1 -- JTBD Analysis, JOB-010**

- **Cited quote:** "Dit portaal zou moeten weten dat ik een chronische patient ben." (P01)
- **Used to support:** The claim that chronic patients want personalized portal experiences with prioritized features.
- **Original context:** P01 made this statement while discussing desired features, as a wish for the future. In context, it is aspirational, not a reaction to the current concept. The finding is valid but the quote supports a desire, not an evaluation of the concept.
- **Correction applied:** Finding retained, but notes updated to clarify this is a forward-looking desire, not a response to existing functionality.

**Example 2 -- Pains & Gains Analysis, PAIN-007**

- **Cited quote:** "Toegankelijker is niet altijd beter. Niet voor alle informatie." (P08)
- **Used to support:** The claim that centralized data presentation increases vulnerability for sensitive health categories.
- **Original context:** P08 made this statement specifically about the medication overview feature, not about the portal in general. The principle is sound, but the quote's scope was expanded beyond its original application.
- **Correction applied:** Context note added to specify that P08's concern was triggered by the medication management feature specifically, and was then generalized by the participant to the portal concept as a whole later in the interview.

### Not Found Quotes

| Quote (as cited)                          | Attributed to | Disposition |

| "Het voelt alsof ik in de wachtkamer zit van mijn eigen leven." | P04 | Removed. P04 used the waiting room metaphor ("wachtruimte zonder ramen") but never said "wachtkamer van mijn eigen leven." |

**Disposition of removed quotes:** All four were replaced with verified quotes from the same participants that support the same findings. No findings were downgraded or removed due to these corrections -- each finding had sufficient verified evidence from other quotes.

### Methodology

Each inference (a claim about user needs, behaviors, or attitudes that goes beyond what participants explicitly stated) was evaluated by 5 independent verification agents against the full transcript corpus. Inferences were classified as:

- **Well-Supported:** Multiple participants provide direct or indirect evidence. The inference follows logically from stated behaviors, expressed emotions, and contextual information.
- **Plausible but Thin:** The inference is reasonable but rests on evidence from only 1-2 participants or requires a interpretive leap that not all agents agreed upon.
- **Unsupported:** The inference contradicts available evidence or has no supporting evidence in the transcripts.

## Findings Reclassified Due to Verification

Three findings were reclassified during the verification process:

### 1. JOB-012 (Care Provider Finder): Downgraded from Medium to Low Confidence

- **Original classification:** 7/20 runs (medium confidence)
- **Reclassified to:** 5/20 runs (low confidence)
- **Reason:** Two of the agent runs that identified this job were using quotes from participants who were describing the feature's limitations, not expressing a personal job. When these runs were re-evaluated, the agents acknowledged they had conflated critique of the feature with expression of a job need. The remaining 5 runs that genuinely identified a user need to understand ranking criteria constitute a low-confidence finding.

### 2. GAIN-006 (Time Savings): Confidence Score Adjusted

- **Original classification:** 10/20 runs (medium confidence)
- **Reclassified to:** 8/20 runs (medium confidence, lower end)
- **Reason:** Two agent runs double-counted evidence -- they cited both P07's "zestig uur per jaar" statement and her "anderhalf uur per week" statement as independent evidence, but these are the same calculation expressed two ways. After deduplication, the supporting evidence base narrowed slightly. The finding remains medium confidence but the score was adjusted.

### 3. PAIN-007 (Privacy Fear): Context Expanded

- **Original classification:** 9/20 runs (medium confidence), category: Functional
- **Reclassified to:** 9/20 runs (medium confidence), category: Emotional
- **Reason:** Verification revealed that the privacy concern was primarily emotional (fear, vulnerability, feeling exposed) rather than functional (data breach risk assessment). P06's fear was about feeling vulnerable as a woman living alone; P08's concern was about stigma around mental health. Reclassifying from Functional to Emotional better represents the evidence.

## Overall Assessment

The analysis outputs are well-supported by the source material. The 77% exact match rate for quotes indicates rigorous sourcing, and the 83% well-supported rate for inferences demonstrates that conclusions are grounded in evidence rather than speculation.

The 4 not-found quotes (3.1%) were all caught during verification and removed before final output. This demonstrates that the verification process is functioning as intended -- it catches hallucinated or fabricated quotes before they reach stakeholders.

The most significant verification finding is the reclassification of PAIN-007 from Functional to Emotional. This changes how the finding should be addressed in design: a functional privacy concern calls for technical controls, while an emotional privacy concern calls for both technical controls and trust-building communication. Both are needed, but the emphasis shifts.

The analysis is recommended for stakeholder presentation with confidence, with the caveat that medium- and low-confidence findings should be presented as hypotheses for further investigation rather than validated conclusions.
