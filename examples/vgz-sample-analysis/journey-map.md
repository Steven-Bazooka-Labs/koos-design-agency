# Journey Map -- Analysis Output

---

## Executive Summary

> This journey map traces 10 VGZ policyholders through eight stages of the concept testing experience, from their current health administration reality through feature-by-feature evaluation to an overall verdict. The emotional arc follows a distinctive pattern: participants begin in frustration (current experience), rise to cautious optimism (first impression), peak at genuine enthusiasm (claims tracking), but then decline through medication management (information without action), hit a low at the care provider finder (confusion and irrelevance), and partially recover during trust assessment before landing at a nuanced overall verdict. Three moments of truth were identified: (1) the claims tracking reveal, where the PostNL metaphor clicked for every participant; (2) the medication management "action gap," where the portal's view-only nature became painfully clear; and (3) the trust assessment, where the digital divide became undeniable. The largest experience gap is between information display and actionability -- the portal shows problems it cannot solve.

## Detailed Journey

### Stage 1: Current Experience (Before Seeing Concept)

#### Touchpoint 1.1: Managing Claims and Administration

**Actions:** Submitting paper or digital claims, calling VGZ for status updates, waiting on hold, maintaining personal tracking systems (spreadsheets, ring binders, Notion boards). Asking family members for help with digital interactions.

**Thoughts:** "Where is my claim? Did they receive it? How long will it take? Should I call again or wait?"

**Emotion:** Frustrated

**Pain Points:**

- Claims disappear into a "zwart gat" (black hole) after submission -- no visibility into progress (PAIN-002)
  > "Je stuurt iets in en je wacht. Je belt en je wacht. Je zit in een wachtruimte zonder ramen."
  > -- Participant P04

- Administrative tasks compete with other life demands for scarce time and energy
  > "Fenna slaapt van twaalf tot twee, dat is mijn enige rustmoment, en die besteed ik liever niet aan in de wacht staan bij VGZ."
  > -- Participant P03

**Opportunities:** The current pain is high enough that even modest improvements will be perceived as significant. The baseline is so low (phone-only, paper-based) that a functional digital portal creates immediate value.

---

#### Touchpoint 1.2: Dealing with Medication Changes

**Actions:** Going to the pharmacy and receiving unfamiliar medication. Reading (or failing to read) the bijsluiter. Calling family members, GPs, or the insurer for explanation. Searching online for information about new medication.

**Thoughts:** "Is this safe? Why was it changed? Who decided this? Does my doctor know?"

**Emotion:** Anxious

**Pain Points:**

- Medication changes happen without advance warning or comprehensible explanation (PAIN-001)
  > "Maar dat had niemand me verteld! Niet VGZ, niet mijn huisarts, niemand."
  > -- Participant P02

- Brand switches for sensitive medications (heart, mental health, pain) cause genuine health anxiety
  > "En dan lig je 's nachts wakker en denk je: stel dat het niet werkt. Stel dat ik een hartaanval krijg."
  > -- Participant P06

**Opportunities:** Proactive medication change communication -- sent weeks before the pharmacy encounter -- could prevent the anxiety entirely. The portal should serve as early warning system, not after-the-fact record.

---

### Stage 2: First Impression (Seeing the Portal)

#### Touchpoint 2.1: Home Screen Evaluation

**Actions:** Looking at the four-block layout. Assessing visual design. Trying to understand navigation. Comparing to familiar interfaces (banking apps, shopping sites).

**Thoughts:** "Is this for me? Can I use this? Where do I start?"

**Emotion:** Cautiously hopeful (PERSONA-A, C) / Apprehensive (PERSONA-B) / Evaluative (PERSONA-D)

**Pain Points:**

- No clear "start here" guidance for digitally less confident users (PAIN-011)
  > "Het lijkt een beetje op de tablet van mijn kleinzoon, hij speelt daar ook spelletjes op met vierkantjes. Maar hij is acht, die snapt dat meteen. Ik zit al te twijfelen en ik heb nog nergens op gedrukt."
  > -- Participant P02

- Immediate question about multi-person access: "Can I switch between my profile and my child's / mother's?" (PAIN-006)
  > "Mijn eerste reactie is: kan ik hierin switchen tussen mijn eigen gegevens en die van Fenna?"
  > -- Participant P03

**Opportunities:** Differentiated onboarding based on user profile. First-time users (PERSONA-B) need step-by-step guidance and reassurance. Returning users (PERSONA-A) need quick access to key functions. Caretakers (PERSONA-C) need immediate profile switching.

---

### Stage 3: Claims Tracking (Feature Walkthrough)

#### ***MOMENT OF TRUTH*** -- Touchpoint 3.1: Seeing the Claims Timeline

> This touchpoint has been identified as a Moment of Truth -- the single most positive reaction across all 10 interviews. It transformed skepticism into enthusiasm for 8 of 10 participants.

**Actions:** Viewing the four-step claims timeline (ingediend, in beoordeling, goedgekeurd, uitbetaald). Reading status of individual claims. Noticing color coding and dates.

**Thoughts:** "This is exactly what I need. This is what I have been asking for. This is like tracking a package."

**Emotion:** Enthusiastic

**Why This Is a Moment of Truth:** All 10 participants responded positively. The PostNL package tracking metaphor was spontaneously used by P01 and then echoed across interviews. Even P02 and P06, who expressed digital hesitancy throughout, acknowledged this would help. P10 said "dit alleen al maakt dit portaal de moeite waard."

**Pain Points:**

- Even at this high point, participants noted the absence of action: "What if a claim is rejected -- can I dispute it here?" (PAIN-003)
  > "Als een declaratie wordt afgewezen, dan wil ik hier meteen bezwaar kunnen maken."
  > -- Participant P03

**Opportunities:** This is the lead feature for MVP launch. It has universal appeal, clear value, and the strongest emotional response. Build it first, launch it first, and use it as the anchor to bring users into the portal. Add expected processing time ("gemiddeld vijf werkdagen, uw declaratie staat op dag drie" -- P07's suggestion) to further reduce uncertainty.

---

### Stage 4: Medication Management (Feature Walkthrough)

#### ***MOMENT OF TRUTH*** -- Touchpoint 4.1: Seeing Medication History and Change Reasons

> This touchpoint has been identified as a Moment of Truth -- the inflection point where enthusiasm turned to frustration. Participants recognized the value of the information but immediately saw its limitation.

**Actions:** Viewing current medications, dosages, and change history. Reading the reason for changes ("preferentiebeleid"). Trying to understand the terminology. Looking for action options.

**Thoughts:** "I can see what changed, but I cannot understand why (jargon), and I cannot do anything about it."

**Emotion:** Conflicted

**Why This Is a Moment of Truth:** This is where the portal's "view-only" limitation became visceral. P01's "vitrinekast" metaphor was coined here. P04's three-month medication switching ordeal made the absence of a dispute button feel personal. P06 was frightened by the word "preferentiebeleid," which she interpreted as the insurer overriding her doctor.

**Pain Points:**

- "Preferentiebeleid" is incomprehensible jargon that triggers anxiety rather than explaining the change (PAIN-004)
  > "Wat is preferentiebeleid? Dat woord ken ik niet. Is dat de verzekeraar die bepaalt wat ik moet slikken? Want dat wil ik niet. Dat mag toch niet?"
  > -- Participant P06

- No way to flag problems, ask questions, or request the original medication (PAIN-003)
  > "Er is geen knop voor bezwaar. Er is geen mogelijkheid om een bericht te sturen aan mijn apotheker of aan VGZ via dit portaal."
  > -- Participant P04

- Change reasons lack plain-language explanation and reassurance (PAIN-001)
  > "Kunt u dat er niet bij zetten, in gewone mensentaal? Zoiets als: 'Uw medicijn is van merk veranderd. Het werkzame bestanddeel is precies hetzelfde. Uw arts is hiervan op de hoogte.' Dan snap ik het."
  > -- Participant P02

**Opportunities:** Three concrete improvements: (1) Replace "preferentiebeleid" with plain-language explanation including "uw arts is akkoord." (2) Add advance notification before changes take effect. (3) Add a "vraag stellen" (ask a question) button that connects to a pharmacist or medical advisor. P02 provided an exact prototype for the replacement text.

---

### Stage 5: Care Provider Finder (Feature Walkthrough)

#### Touchpoint 5.1: Searching and Reviewing Results

**Actions:** Typing a specialty into the search field. Viewing results list with star ratings, addresses, and distances. Trying to understand the ranking logic and rating source.

**Thoughts:** "Why would I use this? My GP recommends specialists. These stars mean nothing to me."

**Emotion:** Confused (PERSONA-B, C) / Indifferent (PERSONA-A, D)

**Pain Points:**

- Unclear ranking criteria -- participants do not know what determines the sort order (PAIN-008)
  > "Waarom staat deze bovenaan? Is dat de beste? De dichtstbijzijnde? De populairste?"
  > -- Participant P03

- Star ratings have no attributed source and are not trusted for health decisions
  > "Sterretjes zonder context zijn decoratie, geen informatie."
  > -- Participant P07

- The metaphor feels wrong -- choosing a doctor should not feel like ordering food
  > "Het voelt als een soort Thuisbezorgd voor artsen, alsof je een arts bestelt, en dat vind ik niet prettig."
  > -- Participant P03

**Opportunities:** If this feature is retained, it needs transparent criteria, condition-specific filtering, and attribution for ratings. However, 7 of 10 participants stated they would not use it, preferring GP recommendations. Consider whether the development investment is justified. P05's assessment: "Lage engagement, hoge development cost."

---

### Stage 6: Referral Letters (Feature Walkthrough)

#### Touchpoint 6.1: Digital Upload Concept

**Actions:** Reviewing the upload interface. Considering how they would photograph a paper referral letter. Evaluating ease of use.

**Thoughts:** "Better than faxing, but why is this my job? My GP should send this directly."

**Emotion:** Mildly positive

**Pain Points:**

- The upload process still requires patient intermediation in a process that should be system-to-system (PAIN-010)
  > "Waarom moet de patient een tussenstation zijn in een proces dat volledig digitaal kan?"
  > -- Participant P01

- Digitally less confident users are unsure how to photograph documents clearly
  > "Peter heeft me een keer laten zien hoe je een foto maakt met de telefoon, maar dat was van de kleinkinderen, niet van een document."
  > -- Participant P06

**Opportunities:** Short-term: provide clear photo instructions ("maak een duidelijke foto, let op dat alle tekst leesbaar is" -- P09). Long-term: automated referral letter transfer via Zorgdomein or LSP, eliminating patient involvement entirely. The upload button is a valid MVP feature but should be positioned as temporary.

---

### Stage 7: Trust & Privacy Assessment

#### ***MOMENT OF TRUTH*** -- Touchpoint 7.1: Discussion of Health Data in Digital Systems

> This touchpoint has been identified as a Moment of Truth -- it revealed the deepest divide among participants and exposed assumptions about who the portal is designed for.

**Actions:** Discussing comfort with health data in an online portal. Evaluating security, privacy, and data handling. Considering who else might see their information.

**Thoughts:** Ranges from "dat schip is allang gevaren" (P01) to "daar ben ik bang voor, echt bang" (P06).

**Emotion:** Divided -- confident (PERSONA-A), fearful (PERSONA-B), conditional (PERSONA-D)

**Why This Is a Moment of Truth:** The trust question revealed that participants have fundamentally different relationships with digital health data. P01, P05, P07, and P09 accepted digital data as normal. P06 was genuinely frightened. P08 was willing but only with granular controls. This division determines whether the portal serves all policyholders or only the digitally comfortable.

**Pain Points:**

- Centralized health data increases exposure risk for participants with sensitive conditions (PAIN-007)
  > "Een pushbericht met 'GGZ-medicatie gewijzigd' op je lockscreen, terwijl je telefoon op tafel ligt bij een werkoverleg: dat is een nachtmerrie."
  > -- Participant P08

- Older participants who live alone feel physically vulnerable if their health status is known
  > "Als iemand weet dat ik hartfalen heb, en dat ik alleen woon, dan weten ze dat ik kwetsbaar ben."
  > -- Participant P06

**Opportunities:** Privacy must be architecturally embedded, not bolted on. Three design requirements: (1) Category-level visibility controls (hide GGZ, show tandarts). (2) Content-free lock screen notifications. (3) Audit log of data access. Additionally, co-browsing phone support could bridge trust: P06 needs a human voice to feel safe, even if the information is digital.

---

### Stage 8: Overall Verdict

#### Touchpoint 8.1: Synthesis and Willingness to Use

**Actions:** Weighing pros and cons. Comparing portal to current process. Deciding which features they would use and which they would skip. Providing overall recommendation.

**Thoughts:** "It is better than what we have, but it is not finished. It is version 0.5."

**Emotion:** Cautiously positive

**Pain Points:**

- The portal is perceived as incomplete -- information without action (PAIN-003)
  > "Het is een verbetering, absoluut, maar het is niet de sprong die het zou kunnen zijn."
  > -- Participant P01

- The concept works well for some but excludes others, potentially deepening the digital divide
  > "Anders creëer je een tweedelingssysteem waarbij de mensen die het al goed kunnen er beter van worden, en de mensen die het moeilijk hebben er niets mee opschieten."
  > -- Participant P01

**Opportunities:** Position the launch explicitly as "version 1" with a visible roadmap. Communicate that action capabilities, caretaker features, and accessibility improvements are planned. Manage expectations while building trust through iterative delivery.

**Arc Narrative:** The journey begins in frustration rooted in years of opaque processes and phone-based administration. It hits its lowest point at the care provider finder, which feels irrelevant and confusing. A partial recovery occurs at referral letters (functional improvement acknowledged) and trust assessment (where confident users carry the aggregate), before settling at cautious positivity -- participants see value but want more.

## Gap Analysis

| Stage         | Current Experience                     | Desired Experience                     | Gap Severity         | Opportunity                            |
| ------------- | -------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------- | -------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| Current Experience | Phone calls, paper mail, waiting, uncertainty | Digital self-service with real-time information | High | The portal concept directly addresses this gap for digitally capable users |
| First Impression | No digital portal exists | Intuitive, accessible portal with clear starting point | Medium | Differentiated onboarding for different user profiles |
| Claims Tracking | "Zwart gat" after submission; status only via phone | Real-time timeline with status, dates, expected duration | Low (concept addresses this well) | Add expected processing time and action buttons for rejected claims |
| Medication Management | Brief from VGZ weeks after change; jargon; no context | Advance notification in plain language with action options | High | Replace jargon, add advance notification, add dispute/question capability |
| Care Provider Finder | GP recommendation or personal network | Transparent, filterable, condition-specific provider search | Medium | Redesign with transparent criteria and condition filtering, or deprioritize |
| Referral Letters | Fax, post, or informal email | Automated system-to-system transfer (no patient intermediation) | Medium | Short-term: upload button. Long-term: Zorgdomein/LSP integration |
| Trust & Privacy | Data exists in multiple systems, no unified view | Controlled, transparent data presentation with granular privacy | High | Privacy controls as first-class feature; co-browsing support for low-confidence users |
| Overall Verdict | No digital self-service | Complete digital portal with information AND action capabilities | High | Phase 1: information (current concept). Phase 2: action capabilities. Phase 3: personalization. |

**Unique Moments of Truth for This Persona:** The medication management "action gap" is the critical moment -- PERSONA-A's expectation of interactivity is highest, so the view-only limitation is felt most acutely.

### PERSONA-D (The Privacy-Conscious Patient) Variations

| Stage         | How This Persona's Experience Differs                      |
| ------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |

## Recommended Next Steps

1. **Redesign the medication management stage** -- this is the largest experience gap and the most emotionally charged moment of truth. Replace jargon, add advance notification, and plan action capabilities.
2. **Design differentiated onboarding** -- PERSONA-B's first impression moment of truth determines whether they engage at all. A guided, reassuring onboarding flow (with "u kunt niets kapotmaken" messaging) is essential for inclusivity.
3. **Launch claims tracking first** -- it is the strongest moment of truth and the most universally positive feature. Use it as the portal's anchor to drive adoption and build trust.
4. **Reconsider the care provider finder** -- it generated the weakest response and the most confusion. Either significantly redesign with transparent criteria and condition filtering, or defer it to focus resources on higher-impact features.
5. **Develop per-persona journey specifications** -- use the variation analysis to create specific design requirements for each persona's experience, particularly at their unique moments of truth.
