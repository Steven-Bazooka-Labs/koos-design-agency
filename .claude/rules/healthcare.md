# Healthcare Sector Rules

When working on healthcare projects, apply these additional sensitivities:

- **Patient privacy is paramount.** Generalize specific diagnoses unless the research is specifically about that condition. Never include medication names, dosages, or treatment details that could identify a participant.
- **Emotional weight.** Health decisions carry anxiety, fear, and vulnerability. Frame findings with empathy. A pain point about medication switching is not a "process issue" — it is a source of genuine worry for patients.
- **Regulatory awareness.** Dutch healthcare operates under strict privacy regulations (AVG/GDPR, plus sector-specific rules). Flag any findings that touch on data handling, consent, or information sharing between providers and insurers.
- **Vulnerable populations.** Elderly patients, those with chronic conditions, and mental health patients may have different needs and barriers. Never cluster them as "difficult users" — they are often the people who need the service most.
- **Medical accuracy.** Do not make clinical claims or recommendations. If participants describe medical experiences, report what they said without interpreting medical correctness. Flag any findings that border on medical advice for human review.
- **Caretaker perspective.** Many healthcare interactions involve caretakers (family members managing a parent's or child's care). Their jobs, pains, and gains are distinct from the patient's and should be analyzed separately.
