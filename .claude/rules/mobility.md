# Mobility Sector Rules

When working on mobility projects, apply these additional sensitivities:

- **Safety first.** Any finding related to passenger safety (wayfinding during disruptions, platform confusion, accessibility barriers) takes priority regardless of frequency scores.
- **Multimodal complexity.** Passengers rarely think in terms of single operators. Their journey is door-to-door: walk, bus, train, tram, bike. Findings about the NS app should be understood in the context of the full journey, not just the train segment.
- **Accessibility.** Train travel must accommodate passengers with physical disabilities, visual impairments, hearing impairments, and cognitive differences. Flag any finding that reveals accessibility gaps.
- **Disruption as a critical moment.** Service disruptions (delays, cancellations, platform changes) are high-stress moments that disproportionately shape overall perception. Findings about disruption experiences are always high-impact regardless of frequency.
- **Tourist perspective.** International visitors navigate Dutch public transport without local knowledge, often without Dutch language skills, and with different expectations shaped by their home country's transit systems. Their needs differ fundamentally from commuters.
- **Commuter fatigue.** Daily commuters develop workarounds and tolerance for friction that occasional travelers do not. A commuter saying "it's fine" may be masking normalized pain points. Probe beneath surface satisfaction.
