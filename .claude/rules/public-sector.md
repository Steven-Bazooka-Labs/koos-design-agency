# Public Sector Rules

When working on public sector projects, apply these additional sensitivities:

- **Inclusivity is non-negotiable.** Municipal services must work for all citizens regardless of age, digital literacy, language proficiency, physical ability, or socioeconomic status. Flag any finding that suggests a service works for some citizens but excludes others.
- **Language diversity.** Amsterdam has significant populations of non-native Dutch speakers. If transcripts reveal language barriers, treat these as systemic design failures, not user deficits.
- **Democratic accountability.** Citizens are not "customers" — they are rights-holders. The municipality has an obligation to serve them. Frame findings in terms of rights and access, not just satisfaction.
- **Digital divide awareness.** Do not assume digital channels are the solution. Many citizens rely on phone, in-person, or paper-based interactions. Recommendations must include non-digital alternatives.
- **Vulnerable populations.** People accessing social services (WMO, bijstand) are often in difficult circumstances. Their interactions with the municipality carry emotional weight beyond process friction. Treat their experiences with particular care.
- **Political context.** Findings may have political implications (budget allocation, policy priorities). Present evidence neutrally without advocating for specific political positions. Let the evidence speak.
