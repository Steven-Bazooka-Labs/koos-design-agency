# KOOS Sector Overview

KOOS works across six primary sectors. Each sector carries distinct domain knowledge requirements, stakeholder dynamics, and sensitivities that must inform how research is conducted and how findings are handled.

---

## 1. Public Sector

**Description:** Policy development, public service design, and digital transformation for government bodies and public institutions.

**Typical Problems KOOS Solves:**
- Redesigning citizen-facing services to reduce administrative burden
- Translating policy intent into workable service delivery
- Digitizing complex regulatory processes while maintaining accessibility
- Tackling systemic challenges (teacher shortages, permit backlogs, public health crises)

**Key Stakeholders:** Civil servants, policy makers, citizens, advocacy groups, regulatory bodies, municipal councils.

**Domain Sensitivities:**
- **Inclusivity and accessibility:** Public services must work for all citizens, including those with limited digital literacy, disabilities, or language barriers. Never assume a digitally confident user base.
- **Democratic accountability:** Design decisions in the public sector carry democratic weight. Recommendations must be defensible under public scrutiny.
- **Vulnerable populations:** Research frequently involves people in difficult circumstances — welfare recipients, immigrants, people navigating bureaucracy during crises. Handle their data and words with particular care.
- **Privacy:** Government data is subject to strict regulation (GDPR, national equivalents). Assume the highest standard of data protection by default.

**Example Work:** Amsterdam Municipality (teacher shortage reduction), Rijkswaterstaat (permit process redesign), CoronaCheck (national COVID certificate app), Dutch Police.

## 2. Big Tech

**Description:** Super app design, platform experiences, and large-scale digital product development for technology companies operating across multiple markets.

**Typical Problems KOOS Solves:**
- Designing coherent experiences across diverse service verticals within a single platform
- Scaling design systems for massive, culturally diverse user bases
- Balancing business growth metrics with genuine user value
- Navigating the complexity of multi-sided marketplaces

**Key Stakeholders:** Product managers, engineering teams, data teams, business unit leads, end users across multiple countries and cultures.

**Domain Sensitivities:**
- **Scale:** Design decisions affect tens of millions of users. A small friction point multiplied by 50 million users is a large problem. Quantitative signals matter.
- **Cultural diversity:** Users span multiple countries, languages, and cultural norms. Never assume a single cultural context.
- **Business metric pressure:** There is constant tension between optimizing for engagement/conversion metrics and protecting user wellbeing. KOOS navigates this tension rather than ignoring it.

**Example Work:** Careem (50M+ users across MENA), OLX (classifieds platform).

## 3. Healthcare

**Description:** Service design and digital product development for hospitals, health insurers, health technology companies, and patient support organizations.

**Typical Problems KOOS Solves:**
- Redesigning patient journeys to reduce friction and anxiety
- Improving insurer-patient communication and service clarity
- Designing health monitoring or support tools
- Streamlining clinical workflows without compromising care quality

**Key Stakeholders:** Patients, caregivers and family members, clinicians, hospital administrators, insurance case managers, regulatory authorities.

**Domain Sensitivities:**
- **Patient privacy:** Health data is among the most sensitive categories under GDPR and sector-specific regulations. Apply maximum caution.
- **Vulnerable populations:** Patients are often in states of pain, fear, or cognitive impairment. Research findings must reflect this context.
- **Regulatory compliance:** Healthcare services operate within strict regulatory frameworks. Design recommendations must account for what is legally permissible.
- **Emotional weight:** Health decisions carry life-altering consequences. Treat research data from healthcare contexts with the gravity it deserves. Never reduce patient experiences to abstract data points.

**Example Work:** VGZ (health insurance, 13% NPS increase), Santeon (hospital group), CUF (Portuguese healthcare), Eurocross (travel health assistance).

## 4. Mobility

**Description:** Public transport, aviation, automotive, and mobility-as-a-service design.

**Typical Problems KOOS Solves:**
- Improving passenger information and wayfinding across transport modes
- Designing booking, check-in, and disruption management experiences
- Creating multi-modal journey planning tools
- Reducing friction in fleet management and leasing services

**Key Stakeholders:** Passengers and travelers, transport operators, airport authorities, fleet managers, accessibility advocates, safety regulators.

**Domain Sensitivities:**
- **Safety:** Transport is safety-critical. Design recommendations must never compromise operational safety, even indirectly.
- **Accessibility:** Transport must serve passengers with mobility impairments, visual impairments, cognitive disabilities, and those traveling with children or heavy luggage. Accessibility is not an edge case.
- **Real-time reliability:** Transport users depend on accurate, timely information. Disruption scenarios (delays, cancellations, rebooking) are where service quality is most tested.
- **Multi-modal complexity:** Journeys increasingly span train, bus, bike, car, and air. Research must account for handoff points between modes.

**Example Work:** NS (Dutch Railways, 10.1M daily passengers), Dubai Airports, KLM.

## 5. Finance

**Description:** Banking, payments, insurance, and financial service design.

**Typical Problems KOOS Solves:**
- Simplifying complex financial products for consumer understanding
- Redesigning onboarding and KYC (Know Your Customer) flows
- Improving digital banking experiences
- Building trust in digital financial services

**Key Stakeholders:** Retail customers, small business owners, financial advisors, compliance officers, regulators (AFM, DNB, etc.).

**Domain Sensitivities:**
- **Financial privacy:** Financial data reveals intimate details about people's lives. Treat it with the same gravity as health data.
- **Regulatory constraints:** Financial services are heavily regulated (PSD2, MiFID, AML directives). Design solutions must operate within these boundaries.
- **Trust:** Financial services depend on trust. Research insights about trust erosion or friction should be handled carefully — they can have outsized business impact.
- **Financial vulnerability:** Some users are in debt, facing financial hardship, or lack financial literacy. Research with these populations requires extra sensitivity and must not be used to exploit their vulnerability.

**Example Work:** ING, bunq, KPN (financial/telecom services).

---

## 6. Energy

**Description:** Energy transition, utility services, grid management, and sustainability-focused design.

**Typical Problems KOOS Solves:**
- Designing consumer-facing tools for energy management and transition
- Making complex energy infrastructure decisions understandable for non-experts
- Supporting behavior change toward sustainable energy use
- Simplifying grid connection and permit processes

**Key Stakeholders:** Consumers and households, energy cooperatives, grid operators, sustainability officers, regulators, municipal energy teams.

**Domain Sensitivities:**
- **Sustainability:** Energy projects carry environmental significance. Research and design work should respect the urgency of the energy transition without oversimplifying the trade-offs involved.
- **Technical complexity:** Energy systems are technically complex (grid balancing, storage, smart meters). Translate complexity for end users without distorting the underlying reality.
- **Consumer behavior change:** Energy transition requires changes in deeply embedded habits. Research must distinguish between stated intentions and actual behavior — people often overstate their willingness to change.

**Example Work:** Liander (Dutch grid operator).
